About Me

This is what I do.

Throughout my many varied positions, the application of technology has been a constant. I graduated from Oregon State University with a Bachelor’s in Political Science in 2011 having hoped to pursue a law degree. I found a career in law was not my passion after working as a paralegal for a few years, but the theme of applying technology was prevalent. As the resident millennial, my knowledge of web technologies was leveraged to redesign the firm’s website. As a result of my experience, I felt comfortable transitioning to a position of web master for thefitnessoutlet.com where I managed the storefront using HTML, XML, and CSS. I also assisted my mother, a high school teacher and college professor, in managing her online courses and various multimedia materials. I was able to apply everything I had learned when I got the opportunity to work as an Instructional Web Designer at the Academy of Art University.

Creating online coursework using established frameworks enticed me to wonder how exactly all the tools I used were designed and built, how it all worked. I began learning javascript on various free learning websites and found it absolutely intriguing. I knew what I was doing was only scratching the surface, but it motivated me enough to want to learn all javascript could do. I began attending local javascript meetups in San Francisco to gain insight into what I needed to learn next, refining my chops at technical meetups like those hosted by Hack Reactor, the web dev bootcamp. I participated in several preparatory courses by Hack Reactor including Fulcrum, a nine week intensive introduction to basic javascript including challenges such as a reimplementation of the underscore library. From Big-O notation, to algorithms and javascript implementations of computer science data structures, I was enamored at all the learning I had before me, but I knew I needed to commit more time. I decided to leave my position as an Instructional Web Designer to learn javascript and computer science full time. I participated in and won at the 2016 AT&T Shape Hackathon for a project I made involving Honda’s Uni-cub vehicle https://youtu.be/UFWoCMBv7eY. I’ve spent my time learning the basics of MongoDB, Express, Angular, React, and Node.
